## Introduction

As our parents age, it can be difficult to know when they need more help. Many elderly people are proud and independent, often hiding their struggles from family members. This guide will help you recognise the early warning signs that your loved one may need additional support.

## 1. Changes in Sleep Patterns

One of the first signs of declining health in elderly people is a change in sleep patterns. This might include:

- Sleeping significantly more or less than usual
- Waking frequently during the night
- Daytime drowsiness or fatigue
- Difficulty falling asleep

## 2. Reduced Physical Activity

A sudden decrease in daily movement can indicate various health issues:

- Fewer steps taken per day
- Less time spent standing or walking
- Avoiding activities they previously enjoyed
- Taking longer to complete routine tasks

## 3. Changes in Heart Rate Patterns

Resting heart rate and heart rate variability (HRV) can reveal important health information:

- Elevated resting heart rate may indicate infection or stress
- Reduced HRV can signal autonomic nervous system issues
- Irregular patterns might precede cardiac events

## 4. Social Withdrawal

Watch for signs that your loved one is becoming more isolated:

- Declining invitations to social events
- Reduced phone calls with friends and family
- Loss of interest in hobbies
- Reluctance to leave the house

## 5. Memory Issues Beyond Normal Ageing

While some forgetfulness is normal, watch for:

- Forgetting recent conversations
- Difficulty following instructions
- Getting lost in familiar places
- Misplacing items in unusual locations

## 6. Poor Nutrition and Hydration

Signs of nutritional issues include:

- Weight loss or gain
- Empty refrigerator or expired food
- Forgetting to eat meals
- Reduced appetite

## 7. Medication Mismanagement

Elderly people often take multiple medications, leading to potential issues:

- Missing doses
- Taking incorrect amounts
- Confusion about what each medication is for
- Forgetting to refill prescriptions

## 8. Changes in Personal Hygiene

Declining self-care can indicate cognitive or physical decline:

- Wearing the same clothes repeatedly
- Neglecting bathing or grooming
- Unkempt appearance
- Body odour

## 9. Home Environment Changes

The state of your parent's home can reveal a lot:

- Unusual clutter or mess
- Spoiled food in the kitchen
- Neglected housekeeping
- Unpaid bills or unopened post

## 10. Mood and Behaviour Changes

Watch for emotional changes:

- Increased irritability
- Depression or sadness
- Anxiety about health
- Paranoia or suspicion

## Taking Action

If you notice any of these signs, it's important to:

1. **Have an open conversation** with your loved one
2. **Consult their GP** for a health assessment
3. **Consider monitoring solutions** like CareConnect AI to track daily patterns
4. **Explore care options** that maintain independence while ensuring safety
