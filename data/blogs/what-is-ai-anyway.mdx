## The Wearable Revolution in Eldercare

When most people think of smartwatches and fitness trackers, they picture young fitness enthusiasts tracking their workouts. But these devices are quietly revolutionising how we care for elderly family members—without requiring them to change anything about their daily routine.

## The Data Goldmine on Your Parent's Wrist

Modern wearables like Fitbit and Apple Watch collect a wealth of health data around the clock:

### Sleep Metrics
- Total sleep duration
- Time in deep sleep, light sleep, and REM
- Number of awakenings
- Sleep efficiency score

### Activity Data
- Steps taken
- Active minutes
- Hourly movement patterns
- Sedentary time

### Heart Health
- Resting heart rate
- Heart rate variability (HRV)
- Heart rate during activity
- Recovery time after exertion

## Why This Data Matters for Elderly Care

Research shows that changes in these metrics often precede health events:

**Sleep disruption** can be an early indicator of:
- Urinary tract infections (UTIs)
- Respiratory issues
- Pain or discomfort
- Cognitive changes

**Reduced activity** may signal:
- Depression or low mood
- Increased fall risk
- Physical weakness
- Social isolation

**Heart rate changes** can indicate:
- Infections (elevated resting HR)
- Stress or anxiety
- Medication side effects
- Cardiovascular issues

They simply continue wearing the device they already own. The insights come to you automatically.
